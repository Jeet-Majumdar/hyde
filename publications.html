Influence of the substrate on the density and infrared spectra of the adsorbed methanol ice of different thicknesses using molecular dynamics simulation

The article discusses the influence of substrates on the density and infrared spectra of adsorbed methanol ice using molecular dynamics simulations. It highlights the importance of methanol in astrochemistry, its detection through infrared spectra, and the role of substrates like KBr in affecting its properties.

Monthly Notices of the Royal Astronomical Society, 522(3), 3656-3664.


Dielectric Profile and Electromelting of a Monolayer of Water Confined in Graphene Slit Pore

The study investigates the structure, dielectric properties and electromelting of water confined between graphene sheets using molecular dynamics simulations. Six nonpolarizable water models were considered, affecting the dielectric profile and electromelting behavior.

J. Phys. Chem. B 2021, 125, 24, 6670-6680


Does twist angle affect the properties of water confined inside twisted bilayer graphene?

The study explore the effects of rotational misalignment between graphene sheets on the properties of confined water, finding that small angular misalignments do not significantly impair dielectric properties but can affect water dynamics.

J. Chem. Phys. 158, 034501 (2023)
